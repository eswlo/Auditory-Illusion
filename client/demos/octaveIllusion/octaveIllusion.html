Instructions

  • Headphones are required to perceive the illusion; test with a lower volume first if using one.
  • Select the note interval before starting, as it cannot be altered during interaction. To choose other intervals, stop the current session, select, and then start the new session.
  • Volumes can be manipulated while playing, so can the toggle.
  • You may experiment with different volumes and note intervals to see how they affect what you perceive.
  • You may also try toggling to reverse the two channels and observe if your perception persists or changes.

Octave
Illusion

Have you ever seen the optical illusion of a spinning dancer known as Silhouette Illusion? The Octave Illusion — discovered by Diana Deutsch in 1973 — demonstrates a similar theme of perceptual variability auditorily.

Read the instructions and try the interactive demonstration to see what pattern(s) you perceive — do you hear the high tone on the right, on the left, or something else/in-between? For further information, visit Deutsch's research webpage.
